Setup / Onboarding Sequence

Confidence Summary: 100% verified, 0% inferred - confirm, 0% unknown.

End-User Track

Precondition: An administrator has installed or launched Pursuite, created your account, and assigned your

system role and any proposal workspace role.

1. [verified] Confirm Docker Desktop is running if you operate the standalone workstation directly.

2. [verified] Launch Pursuite from the desktop shortcut, Start Menu entry, or portable launcher.

3. [verified] Wait for Pursuite Manager to start the local stack and open the local Pursuite web app.

4. [verified] Sign in with the organization, email, and password provided by your administrator.

5. [verified] If MFA is enabled, enter the current 6-digit authenticator code when prompted.

6. [verified] Open Home and confirm the screen shows your name, role-aware panels, and current pipeline

or work data.

7. [verified] Open Contacts -> Accounts and create or open the customer account you will use for pursuit

work.

8. [verified] Add the relevant contact, then create or open the opportunity associated with the pursuit.

9. [verified] Complete bid/no-bid scoring if your role and process allow it.

10. [verified] Create or open the proposal workspace after the opportunity is approved for pursuit.

11. [verified] Upload the RFP PDF or DOCX in the RFP Shredder, review extracted requirements, edit or

add rows as needed, and confirm the requirement set.

12. [verified] Open the Compliance Matrix after requirements are confirmed and generate the first matrix.

13. [verified] Draft assigned sections, review compliance alerts, resolve comments, and move each section

to the next status when ready.

14. [verified] Assemble/export only after required sections, pricing, attachments, and compliance findings

are ready for final production.

Expected result: You can reach Home, open core records, work inside a proposal workspace, and see

confirmed RFP requirements feeding compliance tools.

Confirm success: Confirm your assigned proposal appears in Proposals, your section assignments are

visible, the RFP requirement set is confirmed, and the Compliance Matrix is usable.

Admin Track

Precondition: You have a supported Windows workstation, Docker Desktop is installed and healthy, and

you have the signed Pursuite installer or portable launcher for the release under test.

1. [verified] Install Docker Desktop current stable release on Windows 10 or Windows 11.

2. [verified] Run Pursuite-Standalone-Setup-1.0.0.exe or

Pursuite-Standalone-Launcher-1.0.0.exe.

3. [verified] Open Pursuite Manager and confirm the Dashboard shows Standalone 1.0 Pilot, version

v1.0.0, and local paths for data, environment, backups, logs, and bundled images.

4. [verified] Click Launch Pursuite and wait for the Manager to prepare %APPDATA%\Pursuite, backfill

.env.production, load bundled images, start the stack, and report healthy services.

5. [verified] Sign in with the first-run Admin account from

%APPDATA%\Pursuite\first-run-admin.txt; rotate the temporary credential immediately

after initial access.

6. [verified] Add at least one backup Admin tied to a named employee, not a shared mailbox.

7. [verified] Review organization settings, display name, domain, timezone, feature flags, and plan

entitlements.

8. [verified] Configure SSO where required and confirm a pilot account can sign in.

9. [verified] Configure MFA policy and confirm Admins and Proposal Managers are enrolled

according to company policy.

10. [verified] Configure approved AI provider credentials and test provider connectivity before enabling

AI-dependent workflows.

11. [verified] Configure CUI/ITAR policy and enable external CUI processing only when the provider

and organization approval are documented.

12. [verified] Invite users, assign least-privilege system roles, and assign workspace roles only where

proposal access is needed.

13. [verified] Publish or approve proposal templates and seed the content library with reusable, approved

material.

14. [verified] Run an initial backup from Pursuite Manager -> Backups after confirming login and basic

object creation.

Expected result: The standalone stack is healthy, named administrators exist, security and AI controls

match policy, and users can begin governed proposal work.

Confirm success: Confirm at least two Admins can sign in, a backup completes, AI provider test succeeds if

enabled, and user roles match the approved access model.

Procedure 1: Create and Qualify a New Pursuit

Trigger/precondition: A new solicitation, customer opportunity, or possible bid enters the pipeline.

1. [verified] Open Pipeline.

2. [verified] Click New Opportunity, From Email, From RFP, or Import according to the source.

3. [verified] Enter or verify the opportunity name, agency or customer, solicitation number, due date,

estimated value, NAICS, set-aside, bid type, and source.

4. [verified] Save the opportunity.

5. [verified] Open the opportunity detail page.

6. [verified] Upload source documents on the Documents tab when they are available.

7. [verified] Complete bid/no-bid scoring.

8. [verified] Submit the bid/no-bid decision for approval when your organization requires executive

approval.

9. [verified] Record the approved, declined, or more-info decision only when your role authorizes that

decision.

10. [verified] Create a proposal workspace only after the pursuit is approved for bidding.

Expected result: The opportunity is active, core metadata is complete, the decision is recorded, and a

proposal workspace exists when the organization intends to bid.

Confirm success: Confirm the opportunity status, decision history, and proposal link are visible on the

opportunity detail page.

Procedure 2: Build a Proposal Workspace From an RFP

Trigger/precondition: The pursuit is approved and the team is ready to structure proposal work.

1. [verified] Open the approved opportunity.

2. [verified] Create or open the proposal workspace.

3. [verified] Confirm proposal name, due date, page limit, target format, bid type, and owner.

4. [verified] Add team members in the Team panel.

5. [verified] Assign each participant the correct proposal workspace role.

6. [verified] Select a published template or manually create volumes and sections.

7. [verified] Open RFP Shredder from the proposal workspace.

8. [verified] Upload the solicitation PDF or DOCX.

9. [verified] Wait for extraction to complete.

10. [verified] Review each extracted requirement, edit incorrect rows, add missing requirements, remove

false positives, and mark low-confidence rows reviewed.

11. [verified] Confirm requirements only after the requirement set is complete; confirmation unlocks

the Compliance Matrix and Autonomous Pipeline.

12. [verified] Open Compliance Matrix and generate the matrix.

13. [verified] Assign proposal sections to writers.

14. [verified] Begin drafting only after requirements, structure, and section ownership are in place.

Expected result: The proposal has a team, structure, confirmed RFP requirements, and a generated

assigned sections.

Procedure 3: Draft, Review, and Approve Proposal Sections

Trigger/precondition: You have an assigned proposal section and the proposal workspace is available.

1. [verified] Open the proposal workspace.

2. [verified] Select your assigned section.

3. [verified] Read the section instructions, page limit, source material, and related RFP requirements.

4. [verified] Draft manually, insert approved content library material, or use AI assistance where permitted.

5. [verified] Review AI-generated content for accuracy, grounding, customer relevance, and compliance

before relying on it.

6. [verified] Save frequently.

7. [verified] Check compliance alerts and page or word guidance.

8. [verified] Move the section to the next status when ready.

9. [verified] Request review or notify the assigned reviewer.

10. [verified] Address comments and requested changes.

11. [verified] Mark the section approved only when the reviewer or Proposal Manager accepts it.

Expected result: Required sections have current content, comments are resolved, and section status

reflects the team's review process.

Confirm success: Confirm the section status, comments, and review state match the team's gate criteria.

Procedure 4: Use Compliance Tools Before Final Review

Trigger/precondition: RFP requirements are confirmed and proposal text has enough content to evaluate.

1. [verified] Confirm the RFP requirement set is confirmed in RFP Shredder.

2. [verified] Open Compliance Matrix.

3. [verified] Generate or refresh the compliance assessment.

4. [verified] Review every requirement for covered, partial, or missing coverage.

5. [verified] Assign missing or weak requirements to the right section owner.

6. [verified] Open Compliance and run a compliance scan when available.

7. [verified] Review critical and warning findings.

8. [verified] Fix proposal text or waive findings only when the waiver is justified and reviewable.

9. [verified] Re-run the scan after major corrections.

10. [verified] Export or save the matrix for review when the team needs evidence.

Expected result: Critical gaps are resolved or formally waived, and the team can explain how each RFP

requirement is addressed.

Confirm success: Confirm the compliance score, matrix coverage, and open findings are acceptable for the

next review gate.

Procedure 5: Assemble, Export, and Close Out a Proposal

Trigger/precondition: Required sections are approved and final production is authorized.

1. [verified] Confirm all required sections are approved.

2. [verified] Confirm compliance findings are resolved or accepted.

3. [verified] Confirm pricing, quote totals, attachments, and supporting files are current before

assembly.

4. [verified] Open the export or assembly panel.

5. [verified] Choose the target format such as DOCX, PDF, or PDF/A.

6. [verified] Start assembly.

7. [verified] Review the generated file.

8. [verified] Download the file before the signed link expires.

9. [verified] Store the final file in the approved repository or submission workflow.

10. [verified] Update proposal and opportunity status.

11. [verified] Record closeout and debrief information after submission or award decision.

Expected result: The team has a final export, proposal status is current, and the opportunity record reflects

the latest business outcome.

Confirm success: Confirm the export history, proposal status, opportunity status, and debrief record are

current.

Trigger/precondition: A user must be invited, changed, deactivated, or reviewed.

1. [verified] Open Admin or the user management area.

2. [verified] Invite the user with the least-privilege system role required for the work.

3. [verified] Add optional request context where your organization tracks approvals.

4. [verified] Assign workspace roles only for proposals the user needs to access.

7. [verified] Retain audit history for deactivated users; do not delete audit records.

Procedure 7: Govern AI Provider and RFP Shredder Controls

Trigger/precondition: AI-assisted drafting, RFP extraction, compliance matrix generation, or autonomous

workflows must be enabled or reviewed.

1. [verified] Open AI Provider settings.

2. [verified] Add or update approved provider credentials.

3. [verified] Test provider connectivity before saving the workflow as ready.

4. [verified] Confirm approved use cases and data-handling policy.

5. [verified] Confirm CUI/ITAR policy before allowing controlled documents to use AI processing.

6. [verified] Enable external CUI processing only when the organization and provider are approved

for the data type.

7. [verified] Review RFP Shredder failures for file type, size, AI provider health, CUI gate, and extraction

status.

8. [verified] Preserve audit events for upload, OCR fallback, requirement edits, confirmation, and CUI

blocks.

Expected result: AI-dependent workflows are available only within approved data and security boundaries.

Confirm success: Confirm AI test success, CUI processing status, RFP Shredder status, and relevant audit

events.

5. [verified] Before restore, confirm approval because restore overwrites the current local database

and uploads.

6. [verified] Select the approved backup file.

Admin Configuration & Governance

Confidence Summary: 100% verified, 0% inferred - confirm, 0% unknown.

End-User Track

Precondition: Your organization has defined role, security, proposal, and data-handling policies.

1. [verified] Follow your assigned role boundaries when creating records, drafting, reviewing, or exporting.

2. [verified] Ask a Proposal Manager or Admin when a control is missing or permission-gated.

3. [verified] Do not route CUI, proposal-sensitive, customer-sensitive, or export-controlled material

outside approved Pursuite and company repositories.

4. [verified] Treat AI output as draft assistance and verify it against source documents before relying on it.

5. [verified] Use Archive rather than Delete where the record carries institutional memory.

Admin Track

Precondition: You are responsible for tenant governance, security, compliance, or operations.

1. [verified] Maintain at least two named Admin accounts and review Admin access quarterly.

2. [verified] Configure SSO, MFA, and MFA reset procedures according to company policy.

3. [verified] Assign users least-privilege system roles and proposal workspace roles.

4. [verified] Configure organization profile, primary domain, timezone, feature flags, and plan entitlements.

5. [verified] Configure AI provider credentials, test connectivity, and review approved use cases before

enabling AI workflows.

6. [verified] Configure CUI/ITAR classification controls, minimum clearance rules, and

external-processing approval only where policy allows.

7. [verified] Publish templates only after review; keep proposal-saved templates as drafts until approved.

8. [verified] Govern content library approval so reusable content is reviewed before writers rely on it.

9. [verified] Export audit evidence for compliance review and restrict access when evidence contains CUI or

sensitive proposal data.

10. [verified] Review storage, AI usage, plan limits, scheduled reports, integration health, and stale

workspaces on the documented cadence.
