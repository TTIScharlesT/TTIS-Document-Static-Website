Thompson Telecom Infrastructure Solutions · Virtual Business System Admin & user guide to the end‑to‑end opportunity lifecycle · v1.0 · June 2026

How the VBS runs work

Every business event flows through the same machine:

  1. An event fires — a Pursuite CRM webhook (opportunity.discovered, award.won, …) or an internal trigger (grid.milestone_complete, safety_incident_log.critical).
  2. COMMAND‑CORE routes it — a deterministic, rule‑based router (not an LLM) reads config/routing_rules.yaml and starts the matching workflow.
  3. The workflow runs its phases — agents execute tasks in parallel (independent work at once) and sequential steps (depends_on gates each step on the prior ones).
  4. A human gate pauses for the CEO — anything consequential (a bid decision, a submission, an award acceptance, an invoice) stops and waits for E. Charles Thompson to approve before it proceeds. The system never bids, signs, submits, or moves money on its own.
  5. On approval, the next workflow is released — on_approve.next chains stages together (e.g. an approved bid decision releases proposal development).

Pursuite is the single system of record for the GovCon pipeline; each stage advances the Pursuite pipeline (identified → bid → proposal → submitted → won). Work is prioritized P1 (immediate / safety / deadline) through P4 (routine).

The CEO's seat. You sit at the human gates — the diamonds in the flowchart. The agents do the analysis, drafting, pricing, compliance, scheduling, and field tracking; you make the go/no‑go calls and authorize anything binding or financial.

The flow at a glance

The lifecycle, stage by stage

Each stage lists its trigger, the workflow that runs, the steps (agent → task), the human gate, and what advances to the next stage.

Stage 1 — Lead / Opportunity Discovery → Bid Decision

Trigger: pursuite_webhook.opportunity.discovered · Workflow: WF_BID_ACQUISITION (P3) · Pursuite: identified → bid

A new opportunity lands from Pursuite (SAM.gov monitoring). The team assesses fit, win probability, feasibility, pricing, and compliance, then packages a bid/no‑bid recommendation.

Steps - Parallel: VANGUARD — strategic fit analysis · STRIKER — bid/no‑bid analysis (pWin, content library) - 1. FIELDCOM — operational feasibility (can we staff/schedule it?) - 2. LEDGER — pricing feasibility (cost, GSA rates, vendors) - 3. ARBITER — SDVOSB set‑aside compliance check - 4. APEX — assembles the final bid decision; records it in Pursuite, advances the stage to bid

🚦 Human gate — bid_decision_review (presented by SIERRA‑1): you review the bid/no‑bid package, pricing, and compliance. - Approve → Bid: releases Stage 2 (WF_PROPOSAL_DEVELOPMENT). - No‑bid: the opportunity is closed; STRIKER logs lessons learned to the content library.

Alternate front door — Capture Planning. For a qualified pursuit (opportunity.qualified), WF_CAPTURE_PLANNING (P2) runs first: CONQUEST (win strategy + competitive intel), SCRIBE (content synthesis), CALC (ROM estimate) → QUILL (capture brief) → FIELDCOM (capacity) → VANTAGE (review) → APEX (resource commitment) → your capture_resource_commitment gate → also releases Stage 2.

Stage 2 — Proposal Development → Submission

Trigger: human_gate.bid_decision_review.approved · Workflow: WF_PROPOSAL_DEVELOPMENT (P2) · Pursuite: proposal → submitted

The proposal is built inside the Pursuite workspace, priced, compliance‑checked, and color‑team reviewed.

Steps - 1. STRIKER — create the Pursuite proposal workspace, add the Ross Low Voltage subcontractor, pull from the content library - 2. STRIKER — draft all narrative sections (technical approach, past performance, management approach) - 3. LEDGER — finalize the price volume - 4. ARBITER — final compliance review - 5. APEX — color‑team review (Pink/Red/Gold) when the review gate is ready

🚦 Human gate — proposal_submission (presented by SIERRA‑1): final review and submission authorization. - Approve: you submit via the agency portal (the system cannot submit for you), then STRIKER marks the submission in Pursuite and advances the stage to submitted.

Supporting workflows here: WF_PROPOSAL_48H_ALERT (P1, 60‑min gate as the deadline nears) · WF_OPPORTUNITY_AMENDED (re‑checks impact, compliance, pricing on a solicitation amendment) · WF_CONTENT_GENERATION (SCRIBE drafts content → CONQUEST review → QUILL integrates) · WF_PWIN_ALERT (if pWin drops below threshold, STRIKER → VANGUARD → APEX decide to continue or withdraw).

Stage 3 — Award

Trigger: pursuite_webhook.award.won · Workflow: WF_AWARD_WON (P2)

🚦 Human gate — award_acceptance (presented by APEX, before any step): you review and accept the contract award.

Steps (after acceptance) - 1. APEX — acceptance authorization; records the award - 2. ARBITER — contract review - 3. LEDGER — contract financial setup; status → active - 4. GRID — project plan creation - 5. FIELDCOM — field deployment brief - 6. ROSS‑PRIME — subcontractor activation

If lost: WF_AWARD_LOST runs instead — STRIKER requests a debrief from the contracting officer and VANGUARD analyzes loss factors; lessons are captured for next time.

Stage 4 — Project Kickoff & Mobilization

Trigger: internal.grid.contract_activated · Workflow: WF_PROJECT_KICKOFF (P2)

Activating the contract spins up project setup across PM, procurement, and safety.

Steps - Parallel: FOREMAN — project plan (WBS, schedule + budget baselines, resource plan) · SUPPLY — material procurement plan (BOM, vendor selection) · SENTINEL — site‑specific safety plan, JHA templates - 1. ANCHOR — site mobilization prep

🚦 Human gate — project_kickoff_authorization (presented by ADMIRAL): you authorize kickoff and field mobilization.

  • 2. ADMIRAL — project kickoff authorization
  • 3. ROSS‑PRIME — subcontractor activation brief

New subs? WF_SUBCONTRACTOR_ONBOARDING — GUARDIAN qualifies the vendor → ARBITER reviews FAR flow‑downs → your subcontract_execution gate → GUARDIAN adds them to the approved vendor list.

Stage 5 — Field Execution

Triggers: daily field activity · Agents: FOREMAN, ANCHOR, IRONHAND, SUPPLY

Day‑to‑day delivery, tracked in the operational project record (visible on the Command Center's Field Operations Projects view): - ANCHOR logs daily production (crew count, footage run, connections, tests). Logs are immutable once submitted. - SUPPLY logs material deliveries against the project budget. - FOREMAN / GRID manage schedule and budget (earned value, variance, forecast).

🚨 Safety is paramount. A critical/high incident (safety_incident_log.critical_or_high) fires WF_SAFETY_INCIDENT (P1) with an immediate human gate — work can stop before any other step. SENTINEL safety holds are absolute; only the CEO can release one.

Stage 6 — Quality Milestone Inspection

Trigger: internal.foreman.milestone_ready_for_inspection · Workflow: WF_QUALITY_MILESTONE (P3)

At each project milestone, quality is formally verified before the work is accepted.

Steps - 1. INSPECTOR — conduct the milestone inspection; generate the punch list; document tests - 2. SENTINEL — director‑level QA review and acceptance - 3. FOREMAN — schedule punch‑list corrections; update project status - 4. ADMIRAL — milestone close notification (only when all NCRs are closed)

Execution and inspection repeat per milestone until the project's scope is complete.

Stage 7 — Invoicing & Billing

Trigger: internal.grid.milestone_complete · Workflow: WF_INVOICING (P3)

A completed, verified milestone triggers billing.

Steps - 1. GRID — deliverable verification - 2. LEDGER — generate the SF‑1034 invoice; FAR Part 31 cost‑compliance check - 3. ARBITER — SDVOSB billing‑entity compliance check - 4. APEX — invoice approval

🚦 Human gate — invoice_submission_authorization (presented by SIERRA‑1): you submit the invoice via the agency portal. - On approve: LEDGER records the submission; SIERRA‑1 adds a payment follow‑up to your calendar.

Stage 8 — Project Completion & Close‑out

Agents: GRID, FOREMAN, ADMIRAL

As the final milestone closes and final billing settles, the project is closed out: - FOREMAN — final punch list, as‑builts, final documentation - GRID — project close‑out and deliverable documentation; budget variance reconciliation - ADMIRAL — operational sign‑off

The opportunity has traveled the full path: lead → bid → proposal → award → kickoff → execution → quality → billing → complete.

Reference

Priorities

  • P1 — immediate / human‑in‑the‑loop (safety, submission deadlines, contracting‑officer comms).
  • P2 — urgent (proposal development, award handling, kickoff).
  • P3 / P4 — routine operational workflows.

Glossary

  • COMMAND‑CORE — the deterministic event→workflow router (rules, not an LLM).
  • Human gate — a hold that requires E. Charles Thompson's approval before proceeding.
  • depends_on — a step waits for the listed steps (or the whole parallel_phase) to finish.
  • Pursuite — the GovCon CRM, the single system of record for the bid pipeline.
  • pWin — probability of win.
  • SF‑1034 — the federal invoice voucher form.
