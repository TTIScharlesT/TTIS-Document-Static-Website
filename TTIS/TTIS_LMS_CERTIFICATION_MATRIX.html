This is a planning-level view of every credential, what a learner must complete to earn it (its course bundle + practical), the standards it certifies against, the discrete competencies it carries, and which deployment job titles require it. Course shells are authored under the R-AA-01 approval gate; competencies beyond the federal/lead set below are added during content development.

5. How a credential is actually earned (the gate behind every ✓)

For an earned cert, eligibility (certification_eligibility) is the full competence picture, recomputed server-side at issue — never self-asserted:

eligible  ==  every curriculum course COMPLETED
          AND final assessment PASSED        (if the curriculum sets one)
          AND practical rubric PASSED         (if the cert sets a practical_rubric_id)

The practical is an assessor-bound, attested, WORM-locked, chain-anchored rubric record — so "passed the quiz = competent" is structurally impossible wherever a practical is required. External certs (OSHA) skip coursework/practical and are recorded by an attestor ≠ the learner. Every issue, revoke, and expiry is chain-anchored; recert fires on each credential's own clock or on a cited standard's revision.
