TSG License Manager End User Guide

Version: RC1
Audience: product customers, licensed users, organization members, and account owners

1. What TSG License Manager is

TSG License Manager is the secure licensing and customer access service used by TTI Solutions products. It helps you sign in to your product portal, view licenses, activate products, manage devices, check updates, and review billing or team information when those features are enabled for your account.

2. What you can do in the portal

  • Set up or reset your customer portal password.
  • View assigned product licenses.
  • Review license status and device limits.
  • Activate a product on a device.
  • Deactivate a device you no longer use.
  • Review product updates, downloads, and extensions.
  • View subscription, invoice, or billing status.
  • Review organization or team membership.

4. First-time password setup

  1. Open the setup email from TTI Solutions.
  2. Select the password setup link.
  3. Confirm the page shows the expected product or portal context.
  4. Enter a new password.
  5. Submit the form.
  6. Return to the product portal.
  7. Sign in with your email address and new password.

Setup links expire and are single-use. If the link is expired or already used, request a new setup or reset link.

5. Signing in

  1. Open your product portal.
  2. Enter the email address associated with your product license.
  3. Enter your password.
  4. Select Sign In.
  5. Wait for the portal dashboard to load.

If sign-in fails, confirm the portal URL, email address, and password. Use password reset if available.

6. Resetting your password

  1. Open the product portal.
  2. Select the password reset option, if available.
  3. Enter your account email address.
  4. Open the reset email.
  5. Select the reset link.
  6. Enter a new password.
  7. Sign in again.

Support should never ask you to send your password.

7. Viewing your license

After sign-in, open the license area or dashboard. Typical license details include product name, product key, plan, status, device limit, active devices, timestamps, and billing/subscription status when applicable.

8. Activating a product

  1. Install and open the product.
  2. Choose the activation or license option.
  3. Enter your product key.
  4. Confirm the device label if prompted.
  5. Submit activation.
  6. Wait for confirmation that activation succeeded.
  7. Restart the product if the app asks you to.

The product contacts TSG License Manager to confirm the product key, license status, product match, device limit, and current device authorization.

10. Refreshing license status

Products may periodically refresh license status while online. Refresh may update license status, device authorization, product plan, enabled modules, available extensions, and billing-related access status.

11. Managing devices

  1. Sign in to the portal.
  2. Open the license or device area.
  3. Review device names, IDs, or labels.
  4. Identify old or unused devices.
  5. Deactivate only devices you no longer use.

Deactivating a device may immediately remove product access from that workstation.

12. Updates, downloads, billing, and teams

Your portal or product may show current version, download links, release notes, extensions, module packs, subscription status, invoice history, billing events, organization name, team members, roles, invitations, and billing owner. Available actions depend on your product, plan, and role.

13. Troubleshooting

Activation deniedCheck product key, internet access, license status, and device limit.
Device limit reachedDeactivate an old device or contact support.
Billing suspensionResolve payment issue or contact billing support.

14. Information to provide support

  • Product name.
  • Account email address.
  • App version and operating system.
  • Device label, if activation is involved.
  • Product key prefix or last few characters.
  • Screenshot or exact error message.
  • Approximate time the issue occurred.

Do not send passwords, full payment card details, API keys, private credentials, or secrets from configuration files.

15. Security best practices

  1. Use a strong password.
  2. Do not share your product key.
  3. Use only official portal and download links.
  4. Sign out on shared computers.
  5. Reset your password if you suspect compromise.
  6. Contact support if you see licenses, devices, or billing records you do not recognize.
