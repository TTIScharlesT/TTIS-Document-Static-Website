TSG License Manager Administrator Guide

Version: RC1
Audience: administrators, support leads, operations owners, release managers, and product owners
Environment focus: GCP staging and production-style operations

1. Purpose

TSG License Manager is the shared licensing, entitlement, activation, customer portal, billing, Stripe webhook, email, and product-integration service for TTI Solutions products.

The administrator role is responsible for keeping the license service accurate, secure, auditable, and ready for product teams to integrate with.

2. Core responsibilities

  • Maintain products, tenants, plans, modules, and entitlements.
  • Manage license lifecycle state and device activation records.
  • Verify customer access and product portal boundaries.
  • Review Stripe price mappings and webhook outcomes.
  • Manage password setup/reset workflows and email delivery evidence.
  • Record release, rollback, backup, and acceptance evidence.

6. Daily operating checklist

  1. Sign in with an approved admin account.
  2. Confirm the expected staging or production environment.
  3. Check readiness/database connectivity.
  4. Review recent runtime errors.
  5. Review auth, billing, Stripe, email, and client events.
  6. Confirm no unexpected product or tenant records were created.
  7. Record unusual findings in the release or operations log.

7. Product setup workflow

Step 1: Verify tenant

Confirm tenant ID, display name, domain/portal hostname, theme, visibility group, and support email.

Step 2: Verify product record

Confirm product ID, product name, tenant ID, key prefix, category group, visibility group, and status.

Step 3: Verify plans, modules, and marketplace extensions

Confirm plan IDs, billing type, device limits, module entitlement mapping, extension IDs, required plans, and download/docs URLs.

8. Customer and license management

A valid license record includes customer, product ID, plan ID, product key, status, device limit, and timestamps.

  1. Customer purchases or receives a license.
  2. License is created with the correct product and plan.
  3. Entitlements are attached.
  4. Customer receives setup/license email when configured.
  5. Customer activates the product.
  6. Product refreshes license state periodically.

9. Customer password setup and reset

  1. Verify customer identity through approved support procedure.
  2. Confirm customer email is tied to the expected record.
  3. Send or regenerate a setup/reset token.
  4. Confirm token expiration and single-use behavior.
  5. Ask the customer to sign in through the correct product portal.

10. Product integration credentials

X-TSG-Product-ID: <product_id>
X-TSG-API-Key: <product API key>

Retrieve credentials from Product Integration Settings and share only through approved secure channels.

11. Stripe, billing, and email operations

stripe_price_id -> product_id -> plan_id

Before acceptance, send a signed Stripe test-mode event, verify signature success, confirm license/customer updates, prove duplicate replay is idempotent, and prove tampered payloads are rejected.

For email, confirm password setup, password reset, license-created, and delivery-event records using secrets from the environment or secret store.

13. Release acceptance checklist

  • Hosted CI passes on the release commit.
  • GCP staging deploy is healthy.
  • Readiness returns database OK.
  • Product portal routing is validated.
  • Product identities are correct.
  • Secrets are stored outside source control.
  • Customer tenant/product isolation is verified.
  • Client API credentials reject missing/invalid headers.
  • Activation, refresh, deactivation, and device-limit tests pass.
  • Stripe signed payload, tamper rejection, and replay idempotency pass.
  • SMTP staging delivery passes.
  • Backup/restore and rollback evidence are recorded.

14. Security rules

  • Never commit secrets.
  • Never share product API keys in unapproved channels.
  • Use HTTPS-only staging and production URLs.
  • Use explicit allowed hosts and secure cookies.
  • Disable development seed credentials in production.
  • Restrict GCP permissions by least privilege.
